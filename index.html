About

Building AI agents that behave just like human beings—i.e., Humanoid Agents—has long been a central goal in AI research. Recent advancements have brought us closer to this goal, whether through humanoid avatars in virtual worlds or humanoid robots in the real world.

Humanoid avatar in the virtual world [1]

Humanoid robot in the real world [2]

Developing such agents is a multi-disciplinary challenge, involving contributions from various fields:

  • Computer Vision: 3D human modeling and motion generation
  • Computer Graphics: character animation and physics-based simulation
  • Robotics: locomotion control, policy learning, and navigation
  • Cognitive Science: social cognition and mind reasoning

There have been many exciting advancements within these fields, and we believe now is the perfect time to create a comprehensive platform for researchers across communities to engage in meaningful dialogue. This workshop aims to facilitate the exchange of ideas, explore interdisciplinary intersections, and identify key challenges that can inspire new insights. Additionally, it will serve as an opportunity to discuss ethical concerns and societal impacts, and strategies to address them through collective effort.
